Profile

David is a senior analyst programmer with data base administration and team leading experience. He has developed this experience over the last thirteen years working in various business sectors and industries. His main development expertise lies in Natural, COBOL and IDEAL programming languages, and ADABAS database - which is paired with Natural both Software AG products. He also holds a NCC certificate in SSADM and has used other information engineering methodologies, such as RAD methodology “System Craft”.

During David’s career in I.T. he has been involve in numerous system developments taking the applications from inception through to implementation and post implementation support, the complete system development life cycle and beyond. He has held positions in various sized teams as a team member, and as a team leader of teams of up to six developers.

David has mainly work on mainframe machine’s ranging from the older DOS VSE to the newer VMS XA operating systems, but also has two years experience using and configuring a UNIX AIX machine ready to load a proprietary written software application and database.

When David first started in I.T. machines were accessed using front-end dumb terminals but with the advent of the PC he was introduced to them and has wide experience of the Microsoft and Lotus office products and has used them for small applications.

Career Summary

David’s career in I.T. started when he took a three month system analysis and design TOPS course; which include COBOL programming, and lead to his first I.T. position as a trainee programmer for a re-insurance company in the City of London.

He has gained numerous and varied experience over his career, working his way to senior A/P, DBA and Team leader type roles. David has taken business and I.T. related courses showing his interest in the business of the companies that have employed him and I.T.

Prior to David’s career in I.T. he work as a Metallurgist and managed a testing laboratory for a non-ferrous material producer in the south of England.
